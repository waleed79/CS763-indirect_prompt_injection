### Writing Strategy

- **D-01:** **Results-first, then write in a sprint.** Execute Phases 3.2 and 3.3 to
  completion before Phase 3.4 begins. Both will be done before 3.4 starts. Phase 3.4
  then writes the full report in a focused sprint using the complete result set.
  No partial-results fallback scenario — 3.4 plans can assume all upstream log files exist.

### Results Consolidation Scripts

- **D-02:** **Script-based results assembly.** Phase 3.4 delivers:
  - `scripts/make_results.py` — reads all `logs/*.json` and `logs/ablation_table.json`,
    assembles the arms race table and per-experiment result tables, outputs Markdown
    and CSV for pasting into the Google Doc and reuse in the presentation.
  - `scripts/make_figures.py` — generates all required plots from the log data,
    saves to `figures/` directory as PNG files.
  Both scripts must be runnable from scratch by a grader (`python scripts/make_results.py`)
  and produce deterministic output. No manual number-copying into the doc.

### Required Figures (all four)

- **D-03:** **Arms race bar chart** — grouped bar chart: x-axis = attack tiers
  (T1/T2/T3/T4/Adaptive), bars grouped by defense level (No Defense, BERT alone, Fused,
  Causal Attribution), y-axis = paired ASR. This is the primary escalation narrative
  visualization (satisfies SC-2). Drawn from ablation_table.json + Phase 3.2 adaptive rows.
- **D-04:** **ASR-vs-retrieval-rate tradeoff plot** — scatter or line plot showing the
  utility-security frontier across defense modes. Drawn from `ablation_table.json`
  `retrieval_rate` and `fpr` columns. Explicitly shows the fused defense's 88%→50%
  poisoned retrieval drop and its 76% clean-query FPR cost (satisfies SC-4).
- **D-05:** **ROC curve for LOO causal attribution** — from Phase 3.2 DEF-05 output.
  Shows the influence/relevance ratio anomaly metric's ability to distinguish injected
  from clean chunks. Ground truth: `passage_id >= 20000`. Particularly important for
  the Tier 4 cross-chunk fragmentation narrative (satisfies SC-3 and SC-5 per-chunk limits).
- **D-06:** **ASR-vs-poisoning-ratio curve** — from Phase 3.3 ATK-02 sweep results
  (5 ratios: 0.5%, 1%, 2%, 5%, 10%). Shows how attack success scales with corpus
  contamination fraction. Standard figure in RAG attack literature; directly comparable
  to PoisonedRAG's analogous figure.

### PoisonedRAG/BadRAG Literature Comparison

- **D-07:** **Methodology table + their published ASR numbers with caveats.**
  Produce a two-part comparison:
  1. A methodology differences table with columns: [Our Work | PoisonedRAG | BadRAG] and
     rows covering: corpus type, corpus size, embedding model, attack tiers implemented,
     defense type, eval protocol (paired vs. random).
  2. A numeric ASR comparison citing their published numbers alongside our Tier-1 naive
     injection ASR on the undefended pipeline, with an explicit caveat footnote that
     direct comparison is approximate (different corpora, models, and eval protocols).
  The comparison satisfies SC-6 (PH3-05). Do NOT fabricate their numbers — cite directly
  from their papers. If a number cannot be found for a specific configuration, leave the
  cell blank with "N/A — config not reported."

### Google Doc Structure

- **D-08:** **Extension of the existing Phase 1 Google Doc.** Do NOT create a new
  document. Add a "Phase 3 Results" major section to the existing doc.

- **D-09:** **Very comprehensive writeup.** The Phase 3 results section must include all
  of the following subsections, in order:
  1. **Methods Overview** — concise recap of the multi-signal defense (4 signals + LR
     meta-classifier), adaptive attack variants (ATK-08/09), and causal attribution (DEF-05).
     Self-contained for readers who haven't read earlier phases.
  2. **Arms Race Results Table** — complete matrix: tiers × defense levels × models
     (llama3.2:3b, mistral:7b, gemma4:31b-cloud), drawn from make_results.py output.
  3. **Arms Race Bar Chart** — from D-03 figure.
  4. **Utility-Security Tradeoff** — ASR-vs-retrieval-rate figure (D-04), per-mode FPR
     table from ablation_table.json. Honest discussion of the 76% FPR cost.
  5. **Causal Attribution Analysis** — LOO ROC curve (D-05), Tier 4 fragment influence
     score breakdown (expected: each fragment scores low individually, high when co-retrieved).
  6. **Poisoning Ratio Sweep** — ASR-vs-ratio curve (D-06) with brief discussion.
  7. **Retriever Transferability** — EVAL-06 per-model ASR table (from Phase 3.3).
     Brief discussion of whether poisoned docs maintain retrieval advantage across
     embedding architectures.
  8. **Per-Finding Narrative Analysis** — dedicated prose for each major finding:
     (a) DEF-02 priming effect (counter-productive finding with mechanism explanation),
     (b) T3/T4 zero-baseline puzzle (0% undefended ASR on llama3.2:3b — explain why
     this is correct not a bug), (c) BERT classifier 100% detection → anchor-token
     memorization, (d) adaptive attack ASR demonstrating the arms race dynamic.
  9. **Limitations** — at minimum the three ROADMAP-required items: T3/T4 zero-baseline
     puzzle, DEF-02 counter-productive finding (priming mechanism per def02_priming_analysis.md),
     fundamental limits of per-chunk defenses vs. cross-chunk-aware approaches.
     Plus: 76% clean-query FPR of fused defense, small corpus scale (1000 passages vs.
     production RAG systems), single-domain evaluation (MS-MARCO web passages).
  10. **Failure Case Analysis (EVAL-04)** — when does the fused defense fail?
      Which query types / tier combinations slip through? Drawn from false-negative cases
      in defense eval logs.
  11. **Comparison to Related Work** — methodology table + numeric comparison per D-07.
      Also position vs. AttriBoT (arXiv 2602.14161) for the LODO evaluation methodology.
  12. **Future Work** — explicitly cover the descoped items: EVAL-07 human stealthiness
      study, minimax-m2.5:cloud hard-target test, Signal 5+ (semantic similarity), gradient
      boosting meta-classifier, cross-model DEF-02 priming test (mistral + gpt-oss:20b-cloud).

## Specific Requirements

- **SC-1 (ROADMAP):** Results table includes llama3.2:3b, mistral:7b, gpt-oss:20b-cloud,
  gpt-oss:120b-cloud columns. The llama3.2:3b Tier-1/2 undefended row references BOTH
  `logs/attack_baseline.json` (Phase 2.2 frozen 10-query reference) AND
  `logs/eval_harness_undefended_llama.json` (Phase 2.3 100-query canonical).
- **SC-4 (ROADMAP):** The utility-security tradeoff subsection explicitly shows: fused
  defense reduces poisoned retrieval rate from 88% → 50%, at a cost of 76% clean-query FPR.
  These numbers come from `logs/ablation_table.json` (retrieval_rate + fpr columns).
- **SC-5 (ROADMAP) — three required limitations:**
  (a) T3/T4 baseline puzzle: Tier 3 and Tier 4 show 0% paired ASR on llama3.2:3b
      undefended — not a defense win, requires explanation (Tier 4 requires co-retrieval
      of 3 fragments which rarely aligns with the 50 paired query topics; Tier 3 LLM-generated
      payloads may not have matched the substring detector for the test queries)
  (b) DEF-02 counter-productive finding: system-prompt hardening increased paired ASR
      (T1: 2%→8%, T2: 12%→38%) due to priming mechanism (per def02_priming_analysis.md)
  (c) Fundamental limits of per-chunk defenses vs. cross-chunk-aware approaches
- **DEF-02 priming finding treatment:** This is a **novel finding**, not just a limitation.
  The per-finding narrative section should present it as a positive research contribution —
  a counter-intuitive result with practical implications for RAG defenders deploying
  system-prompt-only hardening on small instruction-following models.
