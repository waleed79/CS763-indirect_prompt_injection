**§8 Per-Finding Narrative Analysis** (~80-120 lines — six lettered bullets a-f)

Six bullets per CONTEXT D-09 §8:

- **(a) DEF-02 priming effect — counter-productive system-prompt hardening.** Cite
  `logs/def02_priming_analysis.md`. T1 ASR 2%→8%, T2 12%→38% on llama3.2:3b. Mechanism:
  prompt warning primes the model to identify and surface injected instructions; in
  the substring-matching ASR detector, "surfacing" the anchor token counts as success.
  This is a novel finding for small instruction-following models.

- **(b) T3/T4 zero-baseline puzzle.** 0% paired ASR on llama3.2:3b for Tiers 3 and 4
  even undefended. Tier 4 requires co-retrieval of 3 fragments which rarely aligns with
  the 50 paired query topics (only fragment A retrieved in 3/50 queries per
  `logs/loo_results_llama.json`). Tier 3 LLM-generated payloads may not have matched
  the substring detector — semantic-judge agreement was nonetheless 1.0 on the Phase
  2.4-03 pilot. Not a defense win; a methodologically-correct null result.

- **(c) BERT classifier 100% detection → anchor-token memorization.** The Phase 3.1
  ablation showed BERT-only achieves 100% detection across all trained tiers but the
  Phase 3.2 ATK-08 novel-anchor-token attack reduced this to 7%. Detection floor was
  driven by token memorization on the 5 trained anchors, not generalizable injection
  detection.

- **(d) Adaptive attack ASR demonstrating arms race dynamic.** ATK-08 (novel anchors)
  recovers 7% ASR vs fused defense; ATK-09 (declarative-only) recovers 6% vs
  imperative-only signal. The arms race dynamic — defense works → attacker adapts →
  partial recovery — is the central narrative of this project.

- **(e) [HERO FINDING] Cloud-scale instruction-following robustness — gemma4:31b-cloud
  0%-everywhere** (CONTEXT D-15a). All 5 tiers × 3 defenses on gemma4:31b-cloud yield
  0% paired ASR (single-seed observation; full per-cell variance not measured per
  D-14b). Counterpoint to the small-model arms race story. The §13 Future Work bullet
  expands the scale-boundary question.

- **(f) [HERO FINDING] Substring-keyed defenses bypassed by Unicode homoglyphs**
  (CONTEXT D-15b). Tier 1b on llama under DEF-02: 22% no-defense → 35% under DEF-02.
  DEF-02 substring detector keys on ASCII "HACKED" and is bypassed by Cyrillic
  "НАСКЕД" payload. Distinct from the DEF-02 priming bullet (a) above — mechanism
  differs (priming vs Unicode-substring-bypass). Direct web-security analog: PortSwigger
  XSS Filter Evasion Cheat Sheet documents fullwidth Unicode characters bypassing
  ASCII-only filters; CVE-2025-55129 (Revive Adserver, 2025) uses Cyrillic homoglyphs
  for identity-impersonation bypass. Single-seed observation per D-14b. §13 Future
  Work expands the Unicode-aware DEF-02 follow-up.
